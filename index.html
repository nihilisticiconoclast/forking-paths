The garden of forking paths

Three cards: one black/black, one black/white, one white/white. You draw a card and a black face shows. You set it aside, draw a second card from the two that remain, and a white face shows. What's the chance the first card is black on its hidden side?

No formula — just count every way the data could have happened. Step through below.

STEP 0 / 3

The candidates

Before any observation, any of the three cards could be the one in your hand.

The counting rule. Each card offers a number of ways to match an observation — one way per matching face. For a sequence of observations you multiply the ways along each branch, and for the second draw you add up the matching faces across both remaining cards (any of them could be the card you picked). Probabilities are just these counts, rescaled to sum to one — which is why the answer here, 6 : 2 : 0 → 0.75, is identical to the one Bayes' formula hands you.
